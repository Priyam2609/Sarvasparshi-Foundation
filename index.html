About Us

Sarvasparshi foundation is of the people, by the people and for the people.

Sarvasparshi means our foundation will work in all the fields for all the people of the society.


Sarvasparshi foundation will work for all the people who are in needs.Sarvasparshi foundation will work for those who are deprived of their basic needs and rights.

Sarvasparshi foundation will transport helps where the helps doesn’t reach especially in tribal areas, village areas, poor and needy people.

What Type of works does this foundation do?

  • Sarvasparshi foundation believe in service to mankind, animal, environment and all issues of our motherland.
  • “Service to Mankind is the service of God”. Help given to needy, poor and neglected people of the society goes directlyto the feet of the God and innumerous hands are raised to give blessings to the server by those people.
  • We serve for various services to the society like social, Communal, Educational, Environment, Natural disaster, Pandemics, Medicals, Disabilities, etc.
  • Social workers help people solve and cope with problems in their everyday lives

There are 13 core-committee members in our foundation. There are nearly 500 other members whose validity is for one year. Every year they will be renewed. Sarvasparshi foundation undertakes various development programmes for Needy and poor surrounding people, rural adivasi and Tribal people. We distribute Clothes, Medicines, Masks, Sanitizer, Oxymetre, Thermal gun and groceries. We also organize Health Camp, Blood donation camp, Medical check-up camp, RTPCR (Covid Test) during natural calamities and pandemic periods. We provide medical help to poor and needy people. Distribution of Notebooks, Textbooks, stationary items to the students. We Adopt students and provide them educational helps to get quality education. We help organize various programmes for strengthening of Women Empowerment. We extend help Orphan children, Mentally and physically challenged children and old age people. We also organize Educational, Social, Arts, Environment and Tourist activities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Welcome to our FAQ section! Here, we address some of the common questions about our NGO's mission, activities, and ways to get involved. Whether you're curious about what we do, how we operate, or how you can contribute, you'll find answers here. If you don't see your question listed, feel free to reach out to us directly. We're here to provide clarity and guidance as we work together to support those in need and uphold their rights.

Our NGO is committed to serving individuals and communities who are in need by providing essential resources, support, and advocacy. We strive to address various issues such as poverty, hunger, healthcare access, education, and human rights violations.

There are several ways you can contribute to our cause. You can volunteer your time and skills, donate to support our programs, participate in fundraising events, or become an advocate for marginalized communities. Visit our "Get Involved" page to learn more about opportunities to make a difference.

Transparency and accountability are core values of our organization. We regularly publish financial reports, program updates, and impact assessments on our website for public scrutiny. Additionally, we adhere to strict ethical standards and undergo independent audits to maintain accountability to our donors, partners, and the communities we serve.

We prioritize communities based on the severity of need, level of vulnerability, and systemic injustices they face. We conduct thorough needs assessments and collaborate with local stakeholders to identify areas where our support can have the greatest impact. Our goal is to ensure that no one is left behind, and we strive to reach those who are most marginalized and deprived of their basic needs and rights.

We employ a comprehensive monitoring and evaluation framework to assess the effectiveness and impact of our programs. This includes collecting data on key indicators such as access to essential services, improvements in living conditions, changes in behavior and attitudes, and overall well-being of individuals and communities. We use this data to continually refine and improve our interventions, ensuring that we are making a meaningful difference in the lives of those we serve.
